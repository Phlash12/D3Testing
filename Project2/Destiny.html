Review: Destiny(Rise of Iron)

Reviewed by a player with No Life

Among the stars

Destiny is a game that initially failed to impress but has since grown and iterated on the best parts of its gameplay; A process that has culminated with it's final expansion Rise of Iron.


Astronomical Arsenal

As is tradition at this point, Rise of Iron brings with it a slew of new guns, new armor and even a new sword to fight back minions of the darkness. Improved performance thanks to the latest patch weapons patch means nearly all of the weapon types will offer comparable performance in the Crucible( Destiny's PvP arean) as well as high end PvE activities; speaking of which Rise of Iron brings with it a new one.



Into the depths below.

The Campaign of Rise of Iron will give players a good look into what the powerful nano-virus "SIVA" can do, but the real potential of this threat is best realized in the new Raid , Wrath of the Machine. Six players will experience three boss encounters requiring the best of their cooperative skills to defeat on both normal and heroic modes.



×


Last Hoorah

This being the final expansion of Destiny 1 , Bungie has opted to make the game the most rewarding it has ever been. Loot drops regularly and is better quality the harder the content is that you conquer. The newest iteration(Destiny 2) arrives in fall of 2017 and we can expect to see more guns, more armor and more enemies to face then. But to those looking for a psuedo-mmo on consoles to shoot aliens and collect loot, Rise of Iron is worthy of your time.



I'll see you star-side: 8/10


Destiny by this point has you totally hooked or totally turned off due to it's state at launch. Still it's latest expansion provides great content for the currently enthralled and great catch-up mechanism's and starting points for returning players who are looking to give it another shot before the new game arrives.
