Review: Nioh

Reviewed by a player with No Life

In the land of the Rising Sun

It would be easy to call Nioh a dark souls clone. In fact, many have done just that already, but to do so, to degrade its accompishments on the genre(dubbed "Soulsbourne" or "Souls-like") is a disservice to the game and the industry as a whole. Nioh takes the tried and true elements of resource management, large levels with intricate and interconnected pathways and bosses that seem impossible to kill, and evolves them beyond even the likes of From Softwares Dark souls series. So now that you have an undertanding of what type of game Nioh is lets's look at how it plays.


Familiar territory

Nioh operates in a manner that fans of Dark souls will remember and relish in. Shrines( Bonfires from the Souls series) operate as checkpoints to level your character up, offer unwanted items for useful items like health potions and weapon buffs. As well, players can change their guardian spirit, an animal spirit that gains charge as you kill enemies and allows the player to unleash a god-mode when fully charged, dealing massive damage and impervious to harm. Nioh features 5 weapon classes, and 6 different armor slots. Weapons and armor operate with stats like weight and armor protection along with a myriad of other affixes that change gameplay. Want more gold from enemies? Certain armor can roll with that affix. There are an unknown amount of affixes and they can potentially roll on all gear that drops. Players of loot based games like Diablo and Borderlands will be very familiar with this system of gearing.



Nioh, unlike the souls games, is a mission-based game. You can play a mission with a particular boss , beat it, and run it again on a higher difficulty( known as Twilight Difficulty). This change in difficulty adds new boss mechanics and better items for those brave enough to venture into it.



×

Nioh's bosses are some of the toughest I have ever faced. I've beaten Bloodborne multiple times on New game +(considered by most to be the hardest Soulsbourne game) and Nioh features a wide array of varying bosses from sengoku era fantasy, with fast movesets, and unforgiving mechanics. Some bosses have moves that will one-shot the player if not dodged, some will drain your stamina bar to the point of staggering you into immobolity. What else were we to expect from the studio that brought us the Ninja Gaiden series?


This may take a while

Not all bosses are as memorable or interesting, but for the majority of the time, each boss at the end of a level gave me a monster of the week vibe much like you'd find in an x-files episode. Nioh's bosses are tough but not impossible to kill. The fights rely heavily on a tough and unforgiving style of play that requires the best of the player in order to defeat them, and when you do the journey was well worth it.



A memorable journey to a faraway land: 9/10


Nioh is a tough but rewarding action game that fans of the Souls series as well as newcomers will enjoy. While at times tough as nails, the levels and bosses reward player effort and mastery of the games many fighting styles and options with great loot and even greater feelings of accomplishment when the final blow strikes down a tough boss. While some levels feel too familiar to some others, there is enough variety in the gameplay to barely notice as you cut down foes using the games many options for engaging combat.
