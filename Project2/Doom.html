Review: DOOM(2016)

Reviewed by a player with No Life

Welcome to Hell

Doom is a series that many thought would not ever return. Doom 3 failed to excite fans and Doom 4 was in "development hell" for almost a decade. So much to the surprise of the industry DOOM released in the Spring of 2016, and it was good. The Multiplayer had a beta and from this many wrote the game off as a failed attempt to recapture the old magic of the original, but they would once again be suprised.


Rumblings here and there but nothing concrete until the overwhelming cries were heard. DOOM 2016 was good, not even good it was great. A serious return to form and entry in the franchise and it made old and new fans alike happy and content with the spectacle on offer. Demons, guns, and a seriously killer soundtrack. And with that lets get into the review.


This is my Boomstick


Doom is a game with fantastic weapon design and it is almost to a fault. Every gun in the game has this tactile feedback loop of slick animations and satisfying handling sounds(coupled with the great sound effects of killing demons) that provides this sense of accomplishment everytime you get a kill. There are 10 weapons in Doom but with the upgrade paths that , in one instance, add a burst fire to your shotgun, you never really bother counting them, because each one is essentially two weapons in one. While the weapons are all powerful, not every weapon is created equally. Part of the great gameplay of Doom is that is expects you to learn which guns work best against which enemies, and this is crucial in later levels of the game and required for harder difficulties. Ammo management is a function of the game but is not tedious and solved via chainsaw kills granting ammo.



×

The guns in Doom are just one pillar of the triad of great gameplay, the next being fantastic enemy design and variety

Here be Demons


Guns aren't worth much if you have nothing to shoot at, and in Doom that's never a problem. Doom reprises almost all of the original enemies with fresh takes on their AI and attack patterns. Invisible demons that charge at you, teleporting demonic witches who summons other less Demons to fight for her, a Cyber-Demon with a laser canon mounted to its arm, or just a plain annoying imp. Doom's enemy design complements the weapon variety on offer and even forces players use new weapons if they want to succeed. The enmies in Doom are designed to test your limits and use the level design to provide absolute carnage to the players. You'll get to know them well over the course of the games roughly ten hour campaign. The enemies don't pile on too much in the beginning but once you reach about the halfway mark you've seen them all, but the combinations in which the game spawns them is half the challenge as some enemies are harder to deal with than others, even with the slew of weapons at your disposal.


One hell of a Ride: 8/10


Doom's multiplayer leaves much to be desired, still despite that portion of the game, the singleplayer of the game is one of the best campaigns we've played in a long time, and we can't wait to see what Id does next.
